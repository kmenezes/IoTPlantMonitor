Motivation

By the year 2050, the worldâ€™s population could exceed 10 billion. Feeding that population means a substantial increase in food production and a push for a more sustainable farming infrastructure. The essence of Precision Agriculture is to provide a sensor platform connected to the cloud combined with aerial/satellite imagery to enable data analytics for farmers/decision makers to help reduce the amount of resources (water/nutrients) required. The IOT Plan Monitor is a basic demonstration of sensor data that can be obtained and reported remotely to a server. Combining historical data with machine learning techniques the IOT Plant Monitor could revolutionize the future of agriculture and take a giant leap forward in tackling world hunger.

Sensors

The IOT Plant monitor utilizes two basic Sensors (but is scalable to more to cover acres of land):


1.Soil Temperature Sensor (XCSOURCE 5PCS Temperature Sensor DS18B20, $15.99 from Amazon)


2.Soil Moisture Sernsor (Kuman 5PCS Soil Moisture Sensor Kit, $8.99 from Amazon)


These sensors are common in agriculture to track how farmers utilize their resources (water, fertilizers, nutrients) which affects yeild and profit margins later on. The total cost was around $28 and was programmed with Python.
